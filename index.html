A Story of Redemption

Before I was 28, I lived a dozen lives.

Born in a war-torn country.

Drowned in heroin as a teenager.

Clawed my way out, bloodied, but alive.

Built a business from scratch, making six figures a month.

Lived in penthouses, flew first class, dined with views people only dream of.

And then in a blink, I lost it all.

A crash. A whisper of metal against pavement.

They said I'd never walk again. Said my hands would never move, that life as I knew it was over.

But I serve a God who doesn't listen to odds.

And I don't introduce myself by statistics anymore.

I am not what happened to me.

I'm a man of purpose.

A man of grit, grace, and God.

I walk, not just on my feet but in faith.

I rise, not just from beds but from ashes.

I'm not here to impress you.

I'm here to serve.

To help. To rise and take others with me.

Hi, my name is Roman.

And I'm not finished.
